Combinators


Descendent Combinator (space)

The descendant combinator selects all elements inside another element, at any depth — no matter how deeply nested.

Ex:

Paragraph 1

Paragraph 2

Child Combinator (>)

The child combinator selects only the direct children of an element — not deeper nested elements.

Ex:

Paragraph 1

Paragraph 2

Adjacent Sibling Combinator (+)

The adjacent sibling combinator selects an element that is immediately after another element, on the same level (same parent).

Ex:

Heading

Paragraph 1

Paragraph 2

General Sibling Combinator (~)

The general sibling combinator selects all elements after a specific element on the same parent level, not just the immediate one.

Ex:

Heading

Paragraph 1

Paragraph 2

Paragraph 3
